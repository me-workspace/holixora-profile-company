# Holixora Discovery Call Structure

## Purpose
A repeatable conversation structure for Holixora to use during early client calls, consultation sessions, and qualification discussions.

## Main Goal
Understand the prospect’s business context, operational pain points, system gaps, and potential fit for Holixora’s services.

## Discovery Principle
The call is not just about pitching.
It is about understanding the business clearly enough to recommend the right next step.

That next step may be:
- AI Automation Audit
- follow-up consultation
- workflow automation project
- custom software discussion
- hospitality operations solution
- or a decision not to proceed yet

---

## 1. Recommended Call Structure
### Suggested length
20 to 40 minutes

### Basic flow
1. Introduction
2. Business context
3. Operational pain points
4. Current systems and workflows
5. Business priorities
6. Fit and opportunity
7. Recommended next step

---

## 2. Opening Script
### Template
Thank you for taking the time today.

The goal of this conversation is to understand your business, how your current workflows and systems operate, where friction exists, and whether there may be a practical way for Holixora to help.

I’d like to keep this discussion focused on your current operations, pain points, and priorities so we can identify the right next step.

---

## 3. Business Context Questions
### Objective
Understand what the business is and how it operates.

### Questions
- Can you tell me a bit about your business and what you do?
- What kind of operation are you running today?
- Who are your main teams or departments involved in day-to-day execution?
- What parts of the business require the most coordination?
- What has changed recently in the business, if anything?

---

## 4. Operational Pain Questions
### Objective
Identify friction, inefficiency, and business pain.

### Questions
- Which workflows currently take more time than they should?
- Where do delays or bottlenecks happen most often?
- What work is still being handled manually today?
- Where does information get lost, duplicated, or delayed?
- Which parts of operations feel hardest to manage or monitor?

### What to listen for
- repetitive work
- fragmented communication
- poor visibility
- reporting pain
- coordination gaps
- manual dependency

---

## 5. Current Systems Questions
### Objective
Understand tools, systems, and operational structure.

### Questions
- What systems or tools are you currently using?
- Are there areas where tools do not connect well with each other?
- Are you relying on spreadsheets, manual reports, or messaging for critical workflows?
- Where do you feel your current systems are weakest?
- Do you have visibility into the data and workflows you need?

### What to listen for
- disconnected tools
- manual workarounds
- fragmented system architecture
- weak reporting structure
- poor system adoption

---

## 6. Business Priority Questions
### Objective
Understand urgency and outcome expectations.

### Questions
- What are your main priorities right now?
- If one operational problem could be improved quickly, what would matter most?
- What kind of improvement would create the biggest business value?
- Is your focus more on efficiency, visibility, control, or growth support?
- How urgent is solving this problem for the business?

---

## 7. Qualification Questions
### Objective
Assess fit, readiness, and next-step potential.

### Questions
- Is there already budget or internal support for solving this?
- Who would be involved in making a decision?
- Are you looking for a quick improvement, a structured audit, or a broader long-term system?
- Have you already explored other solutions?
- What would make a proposed next step feel worthwhile to you?

---

## 8. What Holixora Should Diagnose on the Call
By the end of the call, Holixora should be able to assess:
- how serious the operational pain is
- where the business is losing time or clarity
- whether the issue is best suited for an audit, automation, software, or a larger systems conversation
- whether the lead is a strong, moderate, or weak fit
- whether there is a realistic path to a next engagement

---

## 9. How to Position Holixora During the Call
Holixora should position itself as:
- operationally focused
- business-minded
- practical
- able to recommend the right level of solution

### Suggested line
We focus on helping businesses improve how they operate through automation, workflow design, and custom software where it creates meaningful value.

### Avoid
- overexplaining technology too early
- sounding like a generic software vendor
- promising a solution before enough information is gathered

---

## 10. Recommended Closing Paths
### Closing Path A — Audit
Based on what you’ve shared, I think the best next step may be an AI Automation Audit so we can properly review the workflows, bottlenecks, and strongest improvement opportunities before implementation.

### Closing Path B — Automation Follow-Up
Based on what we discussed, it seems there is already a clear workflow opportunity. The next step could be a more scoped conversation around what automation implementation would look like.

### Closing Path C — Custom Software Discussion
It sounds like the issue is broader than one workflow and may require a more structured system or software solution. The next step would be to define scope and priorities more clearly.

### Closing Path D — Not Yet / Nurture
It may be useful to stay in touch and revisit this when timing or priorities are more aligned.

- [pain point 3]

I believe the best next step would be **[audit / scoped discussion / proposal / follow-up]**.

## 12. Internal Call Notes Template

- Contact name
- Role
- Industry / segment
- Main business context
- Main pain points
- Current systems used
- Urgency level
- Fit score
- Recommended next step
- Notes / risks / objections

---

## 13. Strong Fit Signals
### Good signs
- clear operational pain
- decision-maker involved
- visible inefficiency
- readiness to improve systems
- business impact is easy to understand
- realistic next-step openness

---

## 14. Weak Fit Signals
### Warning signs
- no clear pain point
- no urgency
- only looking for cheapest generic vendor
- no decision-maker involvement
- too early to define any useful need
- weak alignment with hospitality or operational systems

---

## 15. Discovery Call Rules
### Do
- ask clearly
- listen carefully
- focus on business context
- identify real operational pain
- recommend the right next step, not the biggest sale automatically

### Don’t
- overpitch too early
- dominate the conversation
- assume the solution before diagnosis
- overload with technical language
